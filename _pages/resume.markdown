## Personal Projects

### WebGL Text Rendering Experiment — the seed of this application

A personal project that started from a simple desire: "render text on top of WebGL." I wrote a small parser that reads OpenType font files, extracts glyph outlines, and converts them into polygon meshes. I implemented glyph-metric–based glyph placement, baseline computation, and **affine transformations** in shaders, all by hand.

In the process I touched the layers a browser usually hides — kerning, GPOS / GSUB tables, compound glyphs, hinting — and **typography quietly became a domain I cared about**. That curiosity is the reason I am applying for this position.

I am not a typography expert. But I am someone who has *implemented* font internals with their own hands, and I want to use that as a foothold to enter Adobe's Japanese typesetting world.

### dan-shari-gl — A self-authored WebGL framework (npm, 17★)

The text-rendering experiment above runs on top of my own minimal WebGL framework, **dan-shari-gl**. The framework itself is a thin TypeScript wrapper around WebGL — shader management, buffer abstractions, geometry generation — published on npm, with a companion tool `dan-shari-gl-mesh-generator`.

- GitHub: [https://github.com/kenjiSpecial/dan-shari-gl](https://github.com/kenjiSpecial/dan-shari-gl)
- Demo: [https://kenjispecial.github.io/dan-shari-gl/](https://kenjispecial.github.io/dan-shari-gl/)

The product of an attitude of "not just using libraries, but building one to understand the inside." It is the foundation for the text-rendering experiment above and for my other experimental graphics work.
