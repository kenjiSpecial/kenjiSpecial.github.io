## Personal Projects

### dan-shari-gl — A self-authored WebGL framework

A minimal WebGL framework written in TypeScript — a thin wrapper around shader management, buffer abstractions, and geometry generation, published on npm. A companion tool, `dan-shari-gl-mesh-generator`, is also available. The product of an attitude of "not just using libraries, but building one to understand the inside."

- GitHub: [github.com/kenjiSpecial/dan-shari-gl](https://github.com/kenjiSpecial/dan-shari-gl)
- Demo: [kenjispecial.github.io/dan-shari-gl](https://kenjispecial.github.io/dan-shari-gl/)

### WebGL Text Rendering Experiment

A side project that started from a simple desire — to render text on top of WebGL. I wrote a small parser that reads OpenType font files, extracts glyph outlines, and converts them into polygon meshes. I implemented glyph-metric–based glyph placement, baseline computation, and affine transformations in shaders, by hand. Along the way I touched the layers a browser usually hides — kerning, GPOS / GSUB tables, compound glyphs — and developed an interest in typography as a domain.

### NNVisualizer / CNNVisualizer

Real-time visualization of neural networks and convolutional neural networks in the browser.

### infection-simulation

Real-time visualization of an epidemic simulation.
